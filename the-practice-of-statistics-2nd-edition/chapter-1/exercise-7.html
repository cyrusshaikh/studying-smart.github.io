Distribution Description

The dot plot shows that most countries earned zero gold medals, as most of the dots are stacked above 0. This indicates that it was very common for a country not to win any gold medals.

There is one noticeable outlier at 39, which represents a large gap from the rest of the data. This indicates that one country (the United States) earned significantly more gold medals than all the others.

Overall, the data ranges from 0 to 39 gold medals.
