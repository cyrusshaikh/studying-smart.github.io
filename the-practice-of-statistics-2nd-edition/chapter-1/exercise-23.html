Exercise 23

In this problem, we are asked to identify whether each of the listed variables is categorical or quantitative.

Variables:

  • Age: Quantitative, because it represents numerical data (for example, 20 years old).
  • Household Income: Quantitative, since income is expressed in numeric amounts (for example, $25,000).
  • Gender: Categorical, as it classifies individuals into non-numeric categories (such as Male or Female).
  • Political Party: Categorical, because it classifies individuals into groups (such as Republican or Democrat).
Categorical: Gender and Political Party
Quantitative: Age and Household Income
