Exercise 9b

In this part, we are asked to analyze the distribution shown in the stemplot. We will describe its shape, center, spread, and identify any potential outliers.

Shape

Most of the data values are located near the lower end of the stemplot, representing smaller tuition amounts. The distribution extends toward larger values, forming a long right tail. Therefore, the distribution is skewed to the right.

Center

Because the distribution is skewed right, the center will be slightly above the most frequent values. We estimate the center to be approximately $4,500.

Spread

The tuition amounts range from a minimum of $1,300 to a maximum of $19,300.

Outliers

Two data points, $18,200 and $19,300, are noticeably separated from the rest of the data by a gap. These values are considered outliers.
