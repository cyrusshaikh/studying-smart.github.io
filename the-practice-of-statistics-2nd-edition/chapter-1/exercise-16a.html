Exercise 16a

In this problem, we are asked to describe the overall shape of the distribution of total returns based on the provided histogram.

Shape Analysis:

A distribution is considered symmetric when the tallest bars are located near the center, with the bars gradually decreasing on both sides. If the highest bars are toward the left, the distribution is skewed right. If the highest bars are toward the right, it is skewed left.

In this case, the tallest bars appear near the center of the histogram, with smaller bars on both sides forming a balanced shape.

Final Answer: Approximately symmetric
