The given variable is Race, with possible values such as Asian, Black, White, or Other. Our task is to decide whether this is a categorical or quantitative variable.

A categorical variable sorts individuals into distinct groups based on traits or labels — things like color, type, or category. These do not involve any numbers that we calculate or measure. In contrast, quantitative variables involve actual numbers representing measurable amounts.

Since race describes a person’s group membership without using any numerical values, it is clearly a categorical variable.

Final Answer: Categorical
