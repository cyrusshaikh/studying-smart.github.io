Exercise 1b

In this problem, we are asked to identify the variables from the data and classify each one as either categorical or quantitative.

The variables provided in the table are as follows:

  • Make and Model
  • Vehicle Type
  • Transmission Type
  • Number of Cylinders
  • City MPG
  • Highway MPG

Now, let’s classify each:

  • Make and Model: Categorical (this describes names or brands, not numerical values).
  • Vehicle Type: Categorical (describes the category of vehicle, not a number).
  • Transmission Type: Categorical (indicates type of transmission, not numerical).
  • Number of Cylinders: Quantitative (represents a count of cylinders — numerical).
  • City MPG: Quantitative (numerical measurement of miles per gallon in the city).
  • Highway MPG: Quantitative (numerical measurement of miles per gallon on the highway).
Final Answer:

Categorical variables: Make and Model, Vehicle Type, Transmission Type
Quantitative variables: Number of Cylinders, City MPG, Highway MPG
