Exercise 9a

We are provided with a stemplot showing tuition amounts. The stems represent the thousands place of the tuition cost, while the leaves represent the hundreds place. The data has already been rounded to the nearest hundred dollars.

For example, a stem of 24 and a leaf of 3 would represent a tuition of $24,300. Each stem-leaf pair works together to form the full dollar amount, with stems being in thousands and leaves in hundreds.

The stemplot allows us to clearly visualize the distribution of tuition costs in an organized manner.
