Exercise 6a

The data provided shows the number of deaths from various causes. Our task is to calculate what percentage of the total deaths each cause represents, and also find the percentage of deaths due to other causes not listed in the table.

Original Data

Cause of Death Number of Deaths
Motor vehicle accident 42,340
Fall 11,858
Poisoning 10,163
Drowning 4,051
Fire 3,601
Total 92,353

Calculating Percentages

To determine the percentage for each cause, we divide the number of deaths for that cause by the total number of deaths (92,353), and then multiply by 100.

Cause of Death Number of Deaths Percentage
Motor vehicle accident 42,340 45.85%
Fall 11,858 12.84%
Poisoning 10,163 11.00%
Drowning 4,051 4.39%
Fire 3,601 3.90%
Total 92,353 100%

Determining Percentage for Other Causes

The listed percentages add up to:

45.85% + 12.84% + 11.00% + 4.39% + 3.90% = 77.98%

Since the total must equal 100%, the percentage of deaths from unlisted causes is:

100% - 77.98% = 22.02%

Final Answer:
Motor vehicle accident: 45.85%
Fall: 12.84%
Poisoning: 11.00%
Drowning: 4.39%
Fire: 3.90%
Other causes: 22.02%
