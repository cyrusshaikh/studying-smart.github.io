Exercise 17a

In this problem, we are asked to describe the shape, center, spread, and check for possible outliers in the distribution.

Shape:

The highest bars in the histogram are located on the left, while smaller bars form a tail toward the right. This indicates that the distribution is skewed to the right.

Center:

Since the distribution is right-skewed, the center is estimated slightly to the right of the peak. The center is approximately at 2 days.

Spread:

The data values range from about 0 days to 10 days, as indicated by the horizontal axis of the histogram.

Outliers:

There are no noticeable gaps or isolated bars, so no outliers are present in this data set.

Shape: Right skewed    Center: 2 days    Spread: 0 to 10 days    Outliers: None
