High School Chemistry Test – Chapter 14: Acids, Bases, & Titrations

Question 1: Conjugate Acid of HPO₄²⁻

Which of the following is the conjugate acid of HPO₄²⁻?

  • a) H₂PO₄⁻
  • b) PO₄³⁻
  • c) H₃PO₄
  • d) H₃O⁺

Scans:

Blank Question Scan:

Answered Scan with Work:

✅ Correct Answer: a) H₂PO₄⁻

This is the conjugate acid of HPO₄²⁻. H₂PO₄⁻ has one more hydrogen atom and one less negative charge than HPO₄²⁻, which is what happens when a species gains a proton (H⁺).

Explanation: When HPO₄²⁻ accepts one H⁺ ion, it becomes H₂PO₄⁻. That makes H₂PO₄⁻ the conjugate acid of HPO₄²⁻ by the Bronsted-Lowry definition of acids and bases.

❌ Why the Others Are Incorrect:

  • b) PO₄³⁻: This is actually the conjugate base of HPO₄²⁻. It would be formed if HPO₄²⁻ lost a proton, not gained one.
  • c) H₃PO₄: This is phosphoric acid with two extra protons. It is not the immediate conjugate acid of HPO₄²⁻.
  • d) H₃O⁺: Hydronium is a strong acid and not part of the phosphate system. It may react with HPO₄²⁻ but is not its conjugate acid.
