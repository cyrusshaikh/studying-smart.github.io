Question:

Discuss and explain the change in the solar cell current at the different locations tested.

Answer:

The amount of current produced by the solar cell changed depending on the light source, with the highest current measured outdoors. This makes sense because sunlight is the most powerful and direct light source, providing more energy for the solar cell to convert into electricity. In contrast, indoor lighting and phone flashlights produced lower current levels because they are much weaker and less consistent sources of light. Interestingly, the dim hallway produced the second-highest current, which suggests that some natural sunlight still reached the area—but not as much as in full outdoor conditions. These results show how the strength and type of light directly affect how much electricity a solar cell can generate.
