Question:

In the Lab, you use blackberries to make a dye solution for the solar cell. What is the reasoning for using this dye?

Answer:

The blackberry dye is used in the solar cell because it contains natural pigments called anthocyanins, which are excellent at absorbing visible light. When sunlight hits the dye molecules, they absorb photons, causing their electrons to become excited and jump to a higher energy level. These high-energy electrons are then transferred into the conduction band of the titanium dioxide (TiO₂) layer, which acts as a semiconductor.

The TiO₂ provides a large surface area for the dye to attach to and serves as a pathway for the electrons to flow through the external circuit, generating electrical current. This transfer of electrons from the excited dye to the TiO₂ is what initiates the conversion of light energy into electrical energy, making the dye a crucial component in the function of a dye-sensitized solar cell.
