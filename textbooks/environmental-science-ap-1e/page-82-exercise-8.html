Chapter 3

Page 82: Exercise 8

STEP 1
Among all macronutrients, nitrogen is required by the human body in the largest amounts. It plays a critical role as a building block in complex molecules and is essential for forming proteins and amino acids—key components of biological structure and function.

STEP 2
Nitrogen is also vital for the synthesis of nucleic acids, which are used to form DNA and RNA. Because of its central role in supporting cell growth and development, humans need a significant amount of nitrogen to maintain normal biological processes.

STEP 3
Humans absorb nitrogen through compounds that are compatible with their internal biochemistry. These nitrogen compounds—such as ammonium (NH₄⁺) or nitrate (NO₃⁻)—are typically produced by plants, like corn, which convert nitrogen into a usable form during growth.

STEP 4
When humans consume nitrogen-rich foods like corn, they absorb these compounds into their bodies. A portion of this nitrogen becomes part of human tissues, while the rest is eliminated as waste. This incorporation of nitrogen is vital for physical development, and a deficiency can lead to stunted growth or impaired bodily function.

Result: (b)
