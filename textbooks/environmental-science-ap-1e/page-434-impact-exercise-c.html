Chapter 15

Page 434: Exercise c

STEP 1
This question asks us to suggest ways to decrease mercury emissions from power plants that burn coal.

STEP 2
Several strategies can help minimize mercury release from coal-fired power facilities:

• Switch to lower-mercury coal: Using coal with naturally low mercury content will result in reduced emissions during combustion.

• Apply bromine-based treatments: Adding bromine compounds during the burning process helps convert mercury into a more easily captured form, improving removal efficiency.

• Reduce coal dependence: Transitioning away from coal-fired electricity—by improving energy efficiency or switching to renewable sources—can significantly cut mercury output at the source.
