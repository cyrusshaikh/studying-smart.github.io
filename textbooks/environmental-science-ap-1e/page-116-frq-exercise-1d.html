Page 116: Exercise 1d

Thermohaline circulation plays a crucial role in regulating the Earth’s climate by moving warm ocean currents across the globe. This large-scale circulation also supports the transport of nutrients, which is essential for maintaining productive agricultural systems. If this circulation were to shut down, it could lead to uneven heating and cooling across different regions of the planet, disrupting climate stability and causing a breakdown in the natural distribution of nutrients—ultimately affecting agricultural success and ecosystem balance.
