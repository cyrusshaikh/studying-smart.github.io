Chapter 13

Page 375: Exercise 1c

Step 1
There are two main types of land-based hydroelectric power systems:

  • Water impoundment system – This is the most widely used method for generating hydroelectric power. It involves storing water in a reservoir behind a dam. Power output can be controlled by adjusting the opening and closing of the dam's gates, allowing electricity to be generated on demand.

Step 2
Run-of-the-river system – Unlike the impoundment method, this approach does not store large volumes of water. Instead, it channels part of a river’s flow through a small dam or diversion structure before returning it downstream. This setup is generally smaller in scale and produces electricity intermittently, depending on the river’s natural flow rate.
