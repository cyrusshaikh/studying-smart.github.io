Chapter 11

Page 306: Exercise 8

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify the practice that does not align with the principles of Integrated Pest Management (IPM).

STEP 2
Integrated Pest Management is a sustainable farming strategy that combines multiple approaches—like crop rotation, intercropping, and the use of pest-resistant seed varieties—to reduce reliance on chemical pesticides while effectively managing pest populations.

STEP 3
Practices like rotating crops or growing multiple species together interrupt the life cycles of pests, making it harder for them to establish. Supporting habitats for natural pest predators—such as birds and beneficial insects—also helps maintain pest populations at manageable levels. Farmers practicing IPM regularly monitor their crops to detect early signs of pest activity, allowing for targeted and minimal use of pesticides only when absolutely necessary.

STEP 4
However, the complete elimination of pesticides is not a goal of IPM. Instead, pesticides are used strategically as a last resort when other methods aren't sufficient. Therefore, saying that IPM aims to fully eliminate pesticide use is incorrect.

Result: (b) — Completely eliminating pesticides is not a principle of Integrated Pest Management.
