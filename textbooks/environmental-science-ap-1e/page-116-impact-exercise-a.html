Chapter 4

Page 116: Exercise A

STEP 1
Forests around the world provide hundreds of tree species used in the production of paper products. In this example, we’re told that the average person in the United States uses about 661 pounds (300 kilograms) of paper products each year, and 30% of that is specifically used for writing and printing paper.

STEP 2
To find out how much of this total is used just for writing and printing, we need to multiply the total amount of paper used by the percentage that represents these specific paper types.

Annual use for writing and printing = Total paper use × Percentage for writing/printing

STEP 3
Now we plug in the numbers:
Total paper use = 300 kg (661 lb)
Percentage used for writing/printing = 30%, or 0.3 in decimal form
Annual use for writing and printing = 300 kg (661 lb) × 0.3 = 90 kg (198.3 lb)

STEP 4
So, the average American uses approximately 90 kilograms (198.3 pounds) of paper each year just for writing and printing purposes.

Result: 90 kg (198.3 lb)
