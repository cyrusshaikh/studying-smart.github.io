Chapter 19

Page 546: Exercise 6

STEP 1
This question asks us to evaluate the effect on carbon emissions when individuals switch from using private vehicles to public transportation systems like buses.

STEP 2
Buses are more efficient in terms of fuel usage per passenger. Since they carry many passengers simultaneously, the fuel consumption per person is substantially lower compared to single-occupancy vehicles like cars. This directly reduces carbon emissions on a per capita basis.

STEP 3
The energy use per person per kilometer is a useful metric. For cars, this value is approximately 3.6 × 10⁶ joules, whereas for buses it is around 1.7 × 10⁶ joules. This shows that buses require less than half the energy per person per kilometer compared to cars—making them significantly more environmentally friendly.

STEP 4
Shifting from personal vehicles to public transport can greatly reduce fuel consumption and carbon output. Encouraging mass transit usage is a practical step toward reducing greenhouse gas emissions and mitigating climate change.
