Page 23 – Exercise 5

Free-Response Question

Step 1

Natural laws are essential to science because they describe the fundamental principles that govern processes in the natural world. Examples include Newton’s law of gravitation, the laws of thermodynamics, and the law of conservation of mechanical energy.

Step 2

These laws operate consistently and in a single direction, shaping how nature functions. Scientists study these consistent patterns to better understand how different natural systems are interconnected and regulated.

Step 3

Scientific inquiry often leads to the formation of theories. When a theory has been repeatedly tested and confirmed, it can give rise to a natural law. These laws are considered universal—they do not change—and many natural cycles, such as the movement of matter, rely on them. The advancement of science often depends on how well we understand these laws.
