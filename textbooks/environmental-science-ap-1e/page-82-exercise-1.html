Page 82: Exercise 1

STEP 1
Fungi are not considered an abiotic part of an ecosystem—they are living organisms, making them a biotic component.

STEP 2
Every ecosystem or habitat is defined by specific conditions such as temperature, moisture, and light. These conditions are known as environmental factors, and they are categorized into two types: abiotic and biotic.

STEP 3
Biotic factors refer to influences from living organisms, while abiotic factors come from non-living elements of the environment. Examples of biotic components include animals, plants, fungi, bacteria, and vegetation such as trees and flowers. Abiotic factors include sunlight, water, soil, air, temperature, humidity, and minerals.

STEP 4
The types of organisms that can survive in a habitat largely depend on the abiotic conditions present. Climate plays a major role in shaping both the biotic and abiotic aspects of an ecosystem.

Result: (d)
