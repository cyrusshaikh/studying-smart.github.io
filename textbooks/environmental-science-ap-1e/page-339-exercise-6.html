Chapter 12

Page 339: Exercise 6

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify which radioactive element is commonly used as fuel in nuclear power plants for electricity generation.

STEP 2
Among the listed radioactive elements, Uranium-235 (U-235) is the primary fuel used in most commercial nuclear reactors.

U-235 is a non-renewable nuclear fuel that undergoes nuclear fission, a process in which the nucleus splits into smaller fragments when struck by a slow-moving neutron, releasing a large amount of energy in the form of heat.

STEP 3
One reason U-235 is preferred over other isotopes like Uranium-238 is because U-235 can be split by low-energy (thermal) neutrons, making it suitable for controlled chain reactions in reactors.

While elements like Plutonium-239 can also be used in some reactors, controlling the fission process in those cases is more complex. In contrast, U-235 allows for safer and more manageable chain reactions in standard nuclear power facilities.

Result: (a) Uranium-235 — This isotope is widely used in nuclear plants because it can sustain a controlled chain reaction efficiently using low-energy neutrons.
