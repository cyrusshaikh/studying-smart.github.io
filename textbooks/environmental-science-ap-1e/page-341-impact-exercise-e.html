Chapter 12

Page 341: Exercise e

Step 1
Objective:
We need to describe how hybrid vehicles could potentially become carbon-neutral in the future.

Step 2
Pathways to Making Hybrid Vehicles Carbon-Neutral:

Utilizing Synthetic Carbon-Neutral Fuels:
One promising method involves the use of synthetic fuels made from captured carbon dioxide. These fuels are produced by chemically combining CO₂—often drawn directly from the atmosphere or industrial processes—with hydrogen derived from renewable sources. When burned, the CO₂ released equals the amount originally captured, creating a closed carbon cycle.

Blending with Biofuels:
Biofuels made from plant materials or organic waste can also reduce net emissions, especially when blended with synthetic alternatives. The carbon released during combustion was initially absorbed during plant growth, helping offset emissions.

Minimizing Vehicle Use:
In addition to advanced fuels, driving less and relying on alternative transportation—such as public transit, biking, or walking—can help reduce the overall carbon footprint of hybrid vehicles.

Although these technologies are not yet widespread, they offer a future pathway to achieving carbon neutrality in hybrid transportation systems.

Final Answer:
Hybrid vehicles could reach carbon neutrality through innovations like synthetic fuels, biofuel integration, and reduced driving—creating a cleaner transportation future.
