Page 23 – Exercise 9

Free-Response Question

Step 1

In a scientific experiment designed to test caffeine’s effect on heart rate, researchers begin with a null hypothesis that caffeine has no measurable impact on the heart rate of 18-year-old males.

Step 2

To ensure the experiment is valid, scientists aim to remove any outside variables that could influence the results. The goal is to isolate the effect of caffeine by eliminating other factors that could affect heart rate.

Step 3

Variables that might interfere with the outcome include emotional state, health conditions, recent food or beverage intake, room temperature, and general environment.

Step 4

By minimizing or controlling these external influences, researchers can make sure that any observed changes in heart rate are directly caused by the caffeine in the drink—ensuring the results are accurate and dependable.
