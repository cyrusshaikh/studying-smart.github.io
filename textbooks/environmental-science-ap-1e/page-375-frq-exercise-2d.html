Chapter 13

Page 375: Exercise 2d

Step 1
In this subpart, we are asked to explain why one method—either photovoltaic (PV) solar cells or wind turbines—might be more favorable than the other for generating electricity.

Installing wind turbines for home electricity production can be more practical and efficient than using PV solar panels, for the following reasons:

  • Land Efficiency: Wind turbines typically require less land to produce a significant amount of energy, while PV solar systems demand a larger surface area to generate an equivalent amount of electricity.
  • Night Operation: Wind turbines can operate regardless of time of day, as they rely on wind rather than sunlight. In contrast, solar panels are limited to daylight hours and are less effective on cloudy days or during winter months.

Step 2
Additionally, PV solar panels may not be suitable for homes with limited roof or yard space due to the area needed for sufficient energy output. While the upfront cost of wind turbines can be higher, solar panels often require more frequent maintenance, which adds to long-term expenses. This makes wind turbines a more favorable choice for consistent and space-efficient energy generation.

Result:
Wind turbines are generally more advantageous than photovoltaic solar panels due to their smaller spatial footprint, ability to generate power at all hours, and potentially lower long-term maintenance requirements.
