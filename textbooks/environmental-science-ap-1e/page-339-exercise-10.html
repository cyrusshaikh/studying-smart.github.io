Chapter 12

Page 339: Exercise 10

STEP 1
This question asks us to explain what the Hubbert curve predicts.

STEP 2
The Hubbert curve is a bell-shaped graph created by geologist M. King Hubbert. It was developed to predict the production pattern of a finite resource, specifically oil. The curve estimates:

• When oil production will reach its peak (maximum output)
• When it will decline afterward as resources become depleted

The curve typically shows two boundaries:
• An upper limit, representing a high estimate of total reserves
• A lower limit, representing a conservative estimate of reserves

It is most famous for predicting the peak of U.S. oil production in the 1970s, which proved fairly accurate.

Result: (c) — The Hubbert curve predicts when oil production will reach its maximum and when it will begin to decline.
