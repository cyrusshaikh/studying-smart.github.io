Chapter 16

Page 461: Exercise e

STEP 1
This question asks us to calculate how much money could be saved on tipping fees if a household composts common organic and recyclable materials like food scraps, paper, and yard trimmings instead of sending them to the landfill.

STEP 2
From the data provided:

Food scraps = 12.5% of total MSW
Paper and paperboard = 32.7%
Yard trimmings = 12.8%
Together, these materials make up 58% of the total waste generated.

STEP 3
We know from earlier that:

Total waste generated per year = 2.92 tons
Tipping fee = $60 per ton
Tipping cost before composting = $175 per year

STEP 4
Let’s calculate how much waste could be diverted through composting:

2.92 tons × 0.58 = 1.6936 tons ≈ 1.7 tons

STEP 5
If 1.7 tons of waste are composted, then the remaining waste sent to landfill is:

2.92 tons − 1.7 tons = 1.22 tons

STEP 6
Now calculate the new tipping cost after composting:

1.22 tons × $60/ton = $73.20

Compare this to the original tipping cost:
$175.00 − $73.20 = $102.80 savings per year

Result: Savings = $102.80
