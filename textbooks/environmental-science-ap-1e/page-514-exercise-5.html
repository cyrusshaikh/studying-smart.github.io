Chapter 18

Page 514: Exercise 5

STEP 1
While scientists are deeply concerned about the declining genetic diversity of wild species, there is also growing alarm over the loss of genetic variation in domesticated animals and crops that are essential to human survival.

STEP 2
Most of the world’s livestock comes from a limited number of species—specifically, just seven mammal and four bird species. These animals have been selectively bred by humans over time to exhibit certain traits, especially those suited to specific regional climates.

STEP 3
This wide range of traits, which depends heavily on genetic diversity, may be crucial for future breeding efforts. It could allow farmers to adapt livestock to new environmental stresses or emerging diseases.

STEP 4
Unfortunately, commercial agriculture tends to prioritize the most productive and profitable breeds. This narrow focus has led to a significant reduction in genetic diversity within livestock populations.

Result: Correct answer: (a).
