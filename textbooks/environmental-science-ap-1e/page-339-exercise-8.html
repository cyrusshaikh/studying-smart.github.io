Chapter 12

Page 339: Exercise 8

STEP 1
This question asks us to calculate how much time it will take for a radioactive substance to decay from 4 curies to 0.25 curies of activity, given the element’s half-life.

STEP 2
Radioactive decay is the process through which unstable atomic nuclei lose energy by emitting radiation. The half-life of a radioactive substance is the amount of time it takes for half of the original material to decay.

STEP 3
From the problem, we’re given:

Initial radioactivity: 4 curies
Final radioactivity: 0.25 curies
Half-life of the radioactive element: 40 years

We need to determine: How many years it will take to decay from 4 curies to 0.25 curies.

STEP 4
Let’s go through the half-lives:

After 1 half-life (40 years): 4 curies → 2 curies
After 2 half-lives (80 years): 2 curies → 1 curie
After 3 half-lives (120 years): 1 curie → 0.5 curies
After 4 half-lives (160 years): 0.5 curies → 0.25 curies

So, it takes 4 half-lives to reach 0.25 curies.

STEP 5
Each half-life = 40 years
Total time = 4 × 40 = 160 years

Result: (c) — It will take 160 years for the radioactive element to decay to 0.25 curies.
