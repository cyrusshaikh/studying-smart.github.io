Chapter 11

Page 306: Exercise 11

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify the correct definition and description of Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations (CAFOs).

STEP 2
CAFOs are industrial-scale agricultural facilities where large numbers of animals—such as cows, pigs, chickens, and turkeys—are raised in confined spaces. These operations are designed to maximize the production of meat, dairy, and poultry by keeping animals in tightly managed environments. The goal is to increase efficiency, reduce land use, and ensure a consistent supply of feed and care to boost productivity.

STEP 3
While CAFOs are known for generating high volumes of food products and keeping production costs lower, they are also linked to environmental concerns, such as waste management and water pollution. However, from a production standpoint, they are highly efficient.

Result: (a) — This option accurately reflects the purpose and function of CAFOs.
