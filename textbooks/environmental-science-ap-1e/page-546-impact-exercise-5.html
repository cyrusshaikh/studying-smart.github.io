Chapter 19

Page 546: Exercise 5

STEP 1
The earlier estimate calculated global carbon emissions by assuming each person emits the same amount of carbon annually from transportation. This figure was then multiplied by the global population.

STEP 2
This method is incomplete because it ignores many other significant sources of carbon emissions—such as the burning of fossil fuels for electricity and industry, deforestation, agricultural practices, and even biological respiration. Transportation only accounts for about 20–25% of total global carbon output.

STEP 3
Another issue is the false assumption that every individual contributes equally to carbon emissions. In reality, a person's carbon footprint varies widely depending on where they live, the energy sources available in their region, and how often or far they travel by car, bus, or airplane.

STEP 4
Because it overlooks major emission sources and wrongly assumes equal per-person output, the method used in the previous question is not a reliable way to estimate total global carbon emissions.
