Chapter 14

Page 407: Exercise c

STEP 1
We are asked to calculate the ratio of each country’s water footprint to the global average, based on the data provided.

STEP 2
Water Footprint Data (in m³/capita/year):
United States = 2,483
Japan = 1,153
India = 980
China = 702
Global Average = 1,240

STEP 3
Calculate Ratio (Country / Global Average):
United States: 2,483 / 1,240 ≈ 2.00
China: 702 / 1,240 ≈ 0.57
India: 980 / 1,240 ≈ 0.79
Japan: 1,153 / 1,240 ≈ 0.93

STEP 4
The water footprint reflects the volume of water used to produce goods and services consumed by an individual. A higher ratio indicates greater per capita water use, more water-intensive production, and greater potential for water pollution.

Summary:
United States: 2.00
Japan: 0.93
India: 0.79
China: 0.57

Thus, the U.S. has the highest water footprint relative to the global average, suggesting the highest impact per person on global water use.

Result: U.S. = 2.00, Japan = 0.93, India = 0.79, China = 0.57
