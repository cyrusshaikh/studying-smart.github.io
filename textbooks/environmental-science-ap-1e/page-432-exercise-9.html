Chapter 15

Page 432: Exercise 9

STEP 1
We are asked to estimate the typical pH level of rainwater under natural, unpolluted conditions.

STEP 2
Pure water has a neutral pH of 7.0, meaning it is neither acidic nor basic. However, natural rainwater tends to be slightly acidic due to the presence of carbon dioxide (CO₂) and other gases in the atmosphere. These gases dissolve in rainwater and form weak acids, lowering the pH slightly.

STEP 3
Under normal atmospheric conditions, rain typically has a pH between 5.4 and 5.9. This range is still considered natural and not harmful. Rain with a pH lower than 5.4 is categorized as acid rain, which is primarily caused by pollutants such as sulfur dioxide (SO₂) and nitrogen oxides (NOₓ), with SO₂ being the major contributor.

Result: (c)
