Chapter 13

Page 375: Exercise a

STEP 1
This question asks us to compare the total lifetime cost of two types of light bulbs: an incandescent bulb and a compact fluorescent bulb.

STEP 2
Given information:

Incandescent Bulb
Price per bulb: $0.50
Power consumption: 75 watts
Bulb life: 750 hours
Electricity cost: $0.10 per kilowatt-hour (kWh)

Compact Fluorescent Bulb (CFL)
Price per bulb: $5.00
Power consumption: 18 watts
Bulb life: 7,500 hours
Electricity cost: $0.10 per kWh

STEP 3
Incandescent Bulb
Energy used over lifetime: 75 watts × 750 hours = 56,250 watt-hours = 56.25 kWh
Electricity cost: 56.25 kWh × $0.10 = $5.625
Total cost (bulb + electricity): $0.50 + $5.625 = $6.13

STEP 4
Compact Fluorescent Bulb (CFL)
Energy used over lifetime: 18 watts × 7,500 hours = 135,000 watt-hours = 135 kWh
Electricity cost: 135 kWh × $0.10 = $13.50
Total cost (bulb + electricity): $5.00 + $13.50 = $18.50

STEP 5
Even though the CFL costs more over its full lifespan, it lasts 10× longer than the incandescent bulb. A complete comparison would account for needing to buy 10 incandescent bulbs to match one CFL, which would raise the total cost of the incandescent setup well above that of the CFL.

Result: CFL is more cost-effective over time despite higher upfront cost.
