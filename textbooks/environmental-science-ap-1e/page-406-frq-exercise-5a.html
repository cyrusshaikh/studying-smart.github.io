Chapter 14

Page 406: Exercise 5a

STEP 1
In this question, two sources of mercury pollution and their prevention methods have to be explained.

STEP 2
Mercury is a heavy metal whose environmental concentration has increased due to various human and industrial activities. This buildup results in mercury pollution, which poses serious health risks to both humans and wildlife.

STEP 3
Two major sources of mercury pollution:

Combustion of fossil fuels (especially coal):
When fossil fuels are burned for electricity or industrial use, mercury is released into the atmosphere and can enter water systems through deposition.

Incineration of hazardous waste:
Burning medical waste, municipal garbage, or industrial byproducts releases mercury vapor into the air, which eventually settles into water bodies.

STEP 4
Methods to prevent or reduce mercury pollution:

Shift to renewable energy:
Reducing our dependence on coal and other fossil fuels in favor of solar, wind, or hydroelectric power helps cut down mercury emissions at the source.

Upgrade waste treatment technology:
Install and maintain high-efficiency filtration and scrubber systems in waste incinerators to capture mercury before it escapes into the environment.
