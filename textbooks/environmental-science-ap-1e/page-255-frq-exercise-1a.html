Step 1

Look for example figures on the internet, there are so many out there!

Step 2

Cross-sectional diagrams of confined and unconfined aquifers—readily available online—can help illustrate the key structural differences between them. These differences stem from the types of geological materials that make up the surrounding layers and influence how water moves and is stored underground.

Step 3

Confined aquifers are situated between layers of impermeable rock or clay, which trap the water under pressure and allow it to flow more freely through permeable material within the confined space. In contrast, unconfined aquifers are only bounded below by an impermeable layer, with their upper boundary exposed to surface infiltration. As a result, they collect water more slowly, and the surrounding material does not facilitate flow as efficiently.
