Free-Response Question

Step 1

The figure illustrates changes in atmospheric carbon dioxide levels over time. The measured CO₂ concentration serves as an example of an environmental indicator.

Step 2

According to the graph, atmospheric CO₂ levels remained relatively stable for much of history. However, beginning roughly 200 years ago—coinciding with increased human industrial activity—CO₂ levels began to rise sharply and have continued to increase steadily since then.

Step 3

This trend shows the extent to which the atmosphere has been polluted in recent decades. Because it reflects the condition of the air and helps scientists assess environmental health, the current level of CO₂ in the atmosphere is a key environmental indicator.
