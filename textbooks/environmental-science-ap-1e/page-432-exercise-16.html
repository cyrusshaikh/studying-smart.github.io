Chapter 15

Page 432: Exercise 16

STEP 1
This question asks us to determine which types of ultraviolet (UV) radiation are absorbed by the ozone layer in the stratosphere.

STEP 2
Ozone in the stratosphere plays a critical role in shielding life on Earth from the sun’s harmful ultraviolet rays. UV radiation comes in different wavelengths, each with different effects on living organisms and varying levels of atmospheric absorption.

STEP 3
Ultraviolet radiation is classified into three main categories:

UV-A: This type has the longest wavelength and passes through the atmosphere almost entirely. It reaches Earth’s surface and is a known contributor to skin aging and cancer.

UV-B and UV-C: These shorter-wavelength rays are much more dangerous but are mostly filtered out by the ozone layer. In fact, the ozone absorbs nearly all UV-C and about 99% of UV-B radiation, preventing severe biological damage and helping regulate Earth's climate.

STEP 4
Because the ozone layer absorbs both UV-B and UV-C, option (d) is the correct answer.

Result: (d)
