Chapter 5

Page 141: Exercise 6

STEP 1
The northern elephant seal has experienced a significant decline in its population, which has led to a decrease in its genetic variation. Generally, larger populations tend to have greater genetic diversity, meaning they contain a wide range of phenotypes. Conversely, smaller populations have less genetic variation and a narrower spectrum of traits.

STEP 2
Population declines can result from various causes, including climate change (which many animals may struggle to adapt to), the rapid spread of disease, overhunting, or natural disasters such as floods, droughts, fires, or earthquakes.

STEP 3
The bottleneck effect happens when a population sharply decreases due to such events. This reduction limits the genetic diversity passed on to future generations, potentially leading to negative impacts. For instance, a population with low genetic diversity may lose its ability to adapt to environmental or climatic changes.

STEP 4
As a result of this bottleneck, some species may face extinction, while others experience significant population declines but manage to survive.

Result: (d)
