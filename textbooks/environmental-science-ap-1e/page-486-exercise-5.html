Chapter 17

Page 486: Exercise 5

STEP 1
This question asks you to identify which statement correctly describes a type of toxic substance.

STEP 2
Toxic chemicals can impact living organisms in various ways, depending on their classification:

Neurotoxins are substances that disrupt the functioning of the nervous system, often by interfering with nerve signal transmission.

Carcinogens are agents that promote abnormal cell division, potentially leading to cancer.

Teratogens interfere with the normal development of embryos or fetuses. A well-known example is alcohol, which can cause fetal developmental issues, which is why it's discouraged during pregnancy.

STEP 3
Additional categories include:

Allergens, which provoke immune system responses in certain individuals, such as sneezing, skin rashes, or difficulty breathing.

Endocrine disruptors, which mimic or block hormone activity, affecting growth, development, and reproductive systems in both humans and animals.

STEP 4
Among the answer choices, the correct one is (a) because it accurately states that neurotoxins interfere with the nervous system, which aligns with the scientific definition.

Result: (a)
