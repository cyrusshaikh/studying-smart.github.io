Chapter 3

Page 82: Exercise 14

STEP 1
Taxol, an anticancer medication, is an example of an ecosystem service called a provision.

STEP 2
Taxol is derived from the bark of the Pacific yew tree, and it represents one of many natural goods that people can directly obtain from ecosystems. These provisioning services include useful resources such as medicinal plants, food, timber, rubber, animal fur, and raw materials used in construction and manufacturing.

STEP 3
In fact, a large portion of medications prescribed today are based on compounds originally found in plants. In the United States alone, over 150 drugs come from natural sources, many of which are derived from medicinal plants.

STEP 4
Taxol is one such drug, discovered in trees native to the Pacific Northwest forests. It became highly valuable because of its effectiveness and widespread use in cancer treatment. This highlights the importance of preserving biodiversity—not only for ecological reasons, but also because of its potential contributions to medicine and human health.

Result: (c)
