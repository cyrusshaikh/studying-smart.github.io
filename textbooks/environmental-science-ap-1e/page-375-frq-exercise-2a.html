Step 4
(d) Preferred Technology – Solar or Wind?
Assuming other factors such as location and investment costs are considered, wind energy is typically favored when large open land or high average wind speeds are available. Wind turbines generally produce more electricity than solar panels under the right conditions. However, in areas with abundant sunlight and limited wind, PV solar cells may be the better choice. Preference depends on regional climate and energy demand profiles.

(e) Contribution to Energy Efficiency and Conservation:
Yes, both solar panels and wind turbines support energy efficiency and conservation goals. They generate electricity without emitting greenhouse gases or relying on finite fossil fuels. Moreover, they reduce transmission losses when installed close to end users and support decentralization of energy production.
