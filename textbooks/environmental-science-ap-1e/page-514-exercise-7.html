Page 514: Exercise 7

STEP 1
The single-species conservation approach focuses on protecting one species at a time. When a species is identified as being at risk, conservation efforts typically aim to improve its habitat in order to help its population rebound.

STEP 2
This targeted strategy is the foundation of important U.S. legislation, including the Marine Mammal Protection Act (MMPA) of 1972 and the Endangered Species Act (ESA) of 1973.

STEP 3
The MMPA was passed in response to declining numbers of several marine mammal populations. It prohibits the hunting, harming, capturing, or trading of marine mammals and their body parts within U.S. territory.

STEP 4
The ESA authorizes the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service to classify species as either threatened or endangered. Once listed, it becomes illegal to harm, kill, or engage in commerce involving these animals or their body parts.

STEP 5
In addition to legal protections, the ESA empowers the government to purchase land critical for the survival of listed species and to develop species-specific recovery plans to aid in population restoration.

Result: Correct answer: (b).
