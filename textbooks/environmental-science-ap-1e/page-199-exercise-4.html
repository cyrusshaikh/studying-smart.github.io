Chapter 6

Page 199: Exercise 4

STEP 1
As of 2010, China held the largest share of the global population, with 1.3 billion people out of a total of 6.8 billion worldwide. This makes it the most populous country on the planet during that period.

STEP 2
With such a massive population and rapid industrial development, China has become one of the largest contributors to global pollution. A high number of vehicles, factories, and other industrial sources release significant pollutants into the air and water. The larger the population, the greater the demand for energy, transportation, and resources, all of which intensify environmental stress. In response, the Chinese government implemented birth control policies, including the one-child policy introduced in the 1970s, which offered financial incentives to families that limited childbearing.

STEP 3
Although the birth rate has declined over time, China remains the most populous country and continues to require vast amounts of natural resources. If the population continues to grow, environmental pressures will likely increase, including the expansion of industry, increased deforestation, higher vehicle emissions, and intensified use of fossil fuels and consumer products such as air conditioners—leading to even more pollution.
