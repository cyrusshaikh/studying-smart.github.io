Exercise 1b

Step 1

Artesian wells are created by drilling into confined aquifers—underground layers of water-bearing rock or sediment that are sandwiched between impermeable materials. When the well is drilled into this pressurized layer, the internal pressure is released, causing water to naturally rise within the well shaft.

Step 2

The height to which the water rises depends on the balance between the pressure in the aquifer and the weight of the water column in the well. If the pressure is strong enough, the water may flow all the way to the surface without the need for pumping—this is a true artesian well.

Step 3

However, in some cases, the pressure is insufficient to bring water all the way to the surface. These are called sub-artesian wells. In such wells, although water still rises due to pressure, it does not reach the ground level on its own and must be extracted using a pump. This occurs when the water level stabilizes below the upper boundary of the confined layer.
