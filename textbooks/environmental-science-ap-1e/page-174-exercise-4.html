Page 174: Exercise 4

STEP 1
A logistic growth model shows how a population grows over time in a limited environment. In the beginning, when resources are plentiful, the population increases quickly and steadily. But as the number of individuals rises, resources become scarce, and the growth rate begins to slow down. Eventually, population growth tapers off as conditions become less favorable—this slowdown reflects limited food, space, and other factors.

STEP 2
When a population is small, it tends to grow rapidly. But as more individuals are added and population density increases, competition for limited resources slows growth. As the population gets closer to the carrying capacity—the maximum number the environment can support—birth rates decline, death rates may rise, and the overall increase levels out.

STEP 3
The logistic growth curve is often represented as an S-shaped line on a graph. This shape captures how populations grow quickly at first, then gradually level off as they near the carrying capacity. It provides a more realistic picture of population dynamics than exponential models, especially in natural ecosystems.
