Chapter 14

Page 404: Exercise 7

STEP 1
This question asks us to describe the process through which acidic water forms underground.

STEP 2
One source of acidic water is acid deposition, which happens when precipitation (like rain or snow) has a pH lower than 5.5. This usually occurs because nitrogen oxides (NOₓ) and sulfur dioxide (SO₂) are released into the atmosphere by vehicles and industrial activities. These gases react with atmospheric oxygen and water vapor to form nitric acid (HNO₃) and sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄). These acids mix with rain, leading to acid rain, which can seep into the ground and affect water chemistry.

STEP 3
Another significant source of acidic water is abandoned underground mines. In such mines, when pyrite (iron sulfide) comes into contact with air and water, a chemical reaction occurs that produces iron ions and hydrogen ions. The increase in hydrogen ion concentration lowers the pH of the water, making it acidic.

STEP 4
Therefore, acidic underground water can be caused by either acid rain infiltration or geochemical reactions in abandoned mines.

Result: (a)
