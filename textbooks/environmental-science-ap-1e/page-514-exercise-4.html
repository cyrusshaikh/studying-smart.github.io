Chapter 18

Page 514: Exercise 4

STEP 1
Several major forces are responsible for the ongoing loss of biodiversity worldwide. These include habitat destruction, overharvesting, pollution, the spread of invasive species, and climate change.

STEP 2
Among these, human activities such as hunting, fishing, and gathering wild organisms have the most immediate effect on wildlife populations. While many species can withstand some level of harvesting, populations become overharvested when individuals are removed faster than they can reproduce—potentially pushing species toward extinction.

STEP 3
A historical example is the dodo bird on the island of Mauritius. In the 1600s and 1700s, European sailors would stop at the island for supplies and hunted the large, flightless bird extensively.

STEP 4
Within less than 80 years, the dodo was extinct—unable to defend itself from human hunters or invasive species like rats, which humans unintentionally introduced and which preyed on the bird’s eggs. Similar patterns have led to the extinction of other large animals throughout history.

Result: The correct answer is (d).
