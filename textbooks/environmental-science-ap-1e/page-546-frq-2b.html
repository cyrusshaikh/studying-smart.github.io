Chapter 19

Page 546: Exercise 2b

STEP 1
One effective way to reduce methane emissions is to encourage a decrease in red meat consumption. Livestock, particularly cattle, are a major source of methane due to manure and enteric fermentation. These emissions account for roughly 32% of all human-related methane output. Shifting dietary habits toward plant-based or lower-impact protein sources can significantly cut methane production.

STEP 2
Transitioning to green energy is another essential strategy. Technologies like solar panels, wind turbines, and geothermal systems generate electricity with minimal carbon or methane emissions. Reducing our reliance on fossil fuels—especially natural gas, a direct source of methane—helps curb both carbon dioxide and methane contributions to climate change.
