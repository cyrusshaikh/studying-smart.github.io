Chapter 7

Page 201: Exercise a

Step 1:
The table provided outlines the average ecological footprint per person for various regions, including global data and several individual countries. It also lists the population sizes and the total amount of available, usable land in each case.

Step 2:
To fully complete the table, two main calculations are required for each country or region:
(1) the total amount of land needed to support the population's ecological footprint, and
(2) the surplus or deficit of usable land per person, based on the difference between land demand and land availability.

Step 3:
A country’s ecological footprint measures how much land is required to support the resource consumption habits of its people. Looking at the ecological footprint on a per-person basis allows us to understand how living standards and economic prosperity affect environmental strain.

Step 4:
To calculate the total ecological footprint for each country, multiply the average ecological footprint per person (in hectares) by the country’s total population (in millions). This gives the country’s total footprint in millions of hectares.

Step 5:
Applying this formula, we get the following total ecological footprints:

The world: 2.7 hectares × 6,800 million people = 18,360 million hectares
The United States: 9.0 hectares × 1,350 million people = 12,150 million hectares
Japan: 4.1 hectares × 77 million people = 315 million hectares
China: 1.8 hectares × 1,330 million people = 2,394 million hectares
India: 0.8 hectares × 1,170 million people = 936 million hectares

Step 6:
To determine whether a country has a surplus or deficit in usable land, subtract its total ecological footprint from its total usable land area. Then, divide that difference by the population to find the result per person.

Step 7:
Here are the calculated surpluses or deficits in usable land per person:

The world: (11,400 – 18,360) ÷ 6,800 = –1.02 hectares per person
The United States: (1,350 – 12,150) ÷ 307 = –35.1 hectares per person
Japan: (77 – 315) ÷ 128 = –1.85 hectares per person
China: (1,100 – 2,394) ÷ 1,330 = –0.9 hectares per person
India: (468 – 936) ÷ 1,170 = –0.4 hectares per person
