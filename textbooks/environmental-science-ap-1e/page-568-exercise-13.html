STEP 1
Poverty contributes to several environmental and health issues, including poor land management, water contamination, and the spread of diseases. Recognizing the global urgency of these problems, the United Nations outlined major international priorities in the year 2000.

STEP 2
The UN introduced eight primary objectives designed to tackle critical global challenges. These included: fostering international development partnerships, addressing child mortality, promoting women's rights and empowerment, and combating diseases such as malaria and HIV/AIDS.

STEP 3
Additional goals focused on ensuring universal access to primary education, alleviating hunger and extreme poverty, enhancing maternal health, and promoting sustainable environmental practices.

STEP 4
These targets, collectively known as the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs), were established through the UN’s Millennium Declaration. Member nations committed to pursuing these goals with a target deadline of 2015.
