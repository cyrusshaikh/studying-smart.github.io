Page 23 – Exercise 10

Free-Response Question

Step 1

Following the experiment, the most accurate conclusion was that caffeine caused an increase in heart rate among the tested 18-year-old males.

Step 2

Researchers measured each participant’s pulse both before and 30 minutes after consuming the beverage. Those who drank a caffeinated drink showed a noticeable increase in heart rate, indicating that caffeine was the cause of the change.

Step 3

In contrast, those who consumed a decaffeinated beverage experienced only slight changes in heart rate, suggesting that other minor factors—not the drink itself—may have been responsible for those fluctuations.

Step 4

The results supported the well-established effect of caffeine: it raises heart rate after consumption. The reliability of these findings would improve even further with a larger sample size, as this would help minimize random error.

Step 5

Data from the experiment clearly demonstrated that the difference in heart rate before and after drinking caffeine was substantial. This effect was far more pronounced than any changes observed in participants who consumed beverages without caffeine.

Step 6

The minor variations in heart rate among those who drank decaffeinated drinks were likely due to unrelated variables and are not considered significant. However, the consistent and stronger increases in heart rate among those who consumed caffeine strongly support the conclusion that caffeine is the main influencing factor.

Result

(a)
