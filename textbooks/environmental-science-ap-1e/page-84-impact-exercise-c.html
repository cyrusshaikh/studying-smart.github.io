Chapter 3

Page 84: Measuring Your Impact – Exercise C

STEP 1
A carbon footprint calculator was used to estimate the environmental impact of an individual's lifestyle, based on various factors such as transportation habits, energy usage, diet, and waste production.

STEP 2
The assessment projected an ecological footprint of approximately 2 global hectares (gha)—the amount of land required to support the consumption of natural resources. The annual carbon footprint was calculated to be around 2.6 metric tons of CO₂, representing the carbon dioxide emissions linked to daily activities. According to the analysis, carbon emissions made up 45% of the total ecological impact.

STEP 3
The results also indicated that if everyone lived according to this lifestyle, it would take 1.2 Earths to meet global resource demands. The Earth Overshoot Day—the estimated date when annual resource use exceeds what the planet can regenerate—was projected to fall on October 21.

STEP 4
Compared to national averages, these results were significantly lower. For example, in 2017, the average ecological footprint in the United States was reported to be 8.04 gha, with estimates suggesting that the typical American lifestyle would require the resources of 5 Earths to sustain.
