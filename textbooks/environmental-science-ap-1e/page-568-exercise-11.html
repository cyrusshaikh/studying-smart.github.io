Page 568: Exercise 11

STEP 1
Environmental policy implementation generally relies on two major approaches:

Command-and-control, which sets specific limits on pollution and enforces them through penalties or legal measures;

Incentive-based systems, which use financial motivations—like tax incentives or subsidies—to encourage reduced emissions and sustainable choices.

STEP 2
One common financial tool is taxation, which acts as a deterrent by making harmful environmental behaviors more costly. On the other hand, incentives such as rebates or tax credits are used to reward the adoption of environmentally friendly technologies or behaviors.

STEP 3
However, while tools like taxes and subsidies fit clearly into one of the two major categories, pollution permit trading operates under a market-based mechanism, which functions differently from direct enforcement or simple incentives. It establishes a cap-and-trade system rather than a penalty or reward structure.

Result: (c)
