Chapter 11

Page 306: Exercise 10

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify the possible negative consequences of using genetically modified (GM) crops.

STEP 2
Genetically modified organisms (GMOs) are created by directly altering the DNA of plants or animals to enhance specific traits—such as resistance to pests or reduced need for synthetic inputs. These modifications are designed to improve crop yields, reduce chemical usage, and increase disease resistance.

STEP 3
However, despite these benefits, GMOs can also raise environmental and health concerns:

Some individuals may experience allergic reactions from consuming GMO foods, especially if the inserted genes come from known allergens.

There's a risk that crossbreeding between GM crops and wild plant species could transfer modified traits to the natural gene pool. This could lead to a loss of genetic diversity, making ecosystems more vulnerable and reducing the variety of traditional crop species over time.

Result: (d) — This choice correctly identifies the possible adverse effects associated with GM crops.
