Chapter 13

Page 375: Exercise 2e

Step 1
In this subpart, we are asked whether generating electricity through photovoltaic (PV) solar cells or wind turbines promotes energy efficiency and conservation.

Both technologies support energy conservation and enhance efficiency. Solar and wind power are renewable resources, meaning they naturally replenish over time. By relying more on these forms of energy, we reduce our dependence on nonrenewable sources such as coal, oil, and natural gas—which are finite and require millions of years to regenerate.

Step 2
Additionally, energy generated from sunlight and wind contributes little to no air pollution. Incorporating these systems into daily use, especially in residential and commercial buildings, can significantly lower overall energy consumption. For instance, architectural designs that integrate solar heating or passive cooling can reduce the need for artificial temperature control, further promoting sustainable energy use.

Result:
Yes, electricity generation from solar panels and wind turbines contributes to energy conservation and improves overall energy efficiency by minimizing reliance on fossil fuels and reducing environmental pollution.
