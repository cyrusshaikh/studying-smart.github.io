Chapter 10

Page 281: Exercise 1a

STEP 1
Managing this diverse public land would require collaboration among all four key federal agencies: the Bureau of Land Management (BLM), U.S. Forest Service (USFS), National Park Service (NPS), and U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (FWS). Each agency would oversee different aspects based on its area of expertise.

STEP 2
Because the land includes forests, lakes, and grazing areas, it serves multiple ecological and recreational functions. This makes it necessary for all four agencies to participate in its oversight to ensure sustainable and balanced use.

STEP 3
The U.S. Forest Service (USFS) would primarily oversee forest conservation and regulate activities such as selective logging. Meanwhile, the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) would handle recreational planning and the management of pasture use, ensuring that grazing follows sustainable practices.

STEP 4
The Fish and Wildlife Service (FWS) would focus on protecting wildlife in both forest and aquatic environments. This would include regulating hunting and fishing, promoting the recovery of endangered species, and maintaining biodiversity in the lake through fish stocking and population monitoring.

STEP 5
The National Park Service (NPS) would be responsible for maintaining ecological integrity while also supporting tourism and public enjoyment. The agency would ensure that recreational activities are compatible with preserving the area’s natural habitats and any rare or endemic species present.
