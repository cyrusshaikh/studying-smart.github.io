Chapter 20

Page 570: Exercise c

STEP 1
Environmental equality—meaning the fair distribution of natural resources and exposure to environmental risks—has become a major international focus. This issue intersects with sustainability and ethical concerns about fairness, especially as growing populations place higher demands on the planet.

STEP 2
The pressure for improved living standards worldwide intensifies this imbalance. As more people seek the benefits of development, global resources may struggle to meet rising consumption levels sustainably.

STEP 3
Recent research has revealed that environmental hazards are not borne equally. Marginalized populations—including racial minorities and those in lower-income brackets—are more likely to live near pollution sources or hazardous sites.

STEP 4
This imbalance is at the core of environmental justice, a field of study and activist movement that investigates whether environmental regulations are enforced fairly. It challenges the systemic inequalities that lead to unequal exposure to environmental risks across different social and economic groups.
