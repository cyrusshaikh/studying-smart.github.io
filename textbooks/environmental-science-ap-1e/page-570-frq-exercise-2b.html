Chapter 20

Page 570: Exercise 2b

STEP 1
In both environmental and ecological economics, the term valuation refers to the process of assigning financial worth to non-market goods such as ecosystem functions and natural resources. These include services like clean air, water filtration, or biodiversity—benefits that are essential but typically unpriced in conventional markets.

STEP 2
If producers were required to account for the full environmental costs of resource use—essentially internalizing externalities like habitat loss or pollution—the cost of producing goods would rise. This increase would likely be reflected in the final price passed on to consumers.

STEP 3
As a consequence, items that cause significant ecological harm would become more expensive, which would discourage their purchase. Over time, this could shift demand toward more sustainable alternatives with lower environmental impact.
