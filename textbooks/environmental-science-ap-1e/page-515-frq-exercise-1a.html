Page 515: Exercise 1a

STEP 1
The Endangered Species Act (ESA) is a foundational U.S. law designed to halt the disappearance of plant and animal species facing extinction. It was established to provide legal protection and recovery measures for species at risk due to human activity or environmental threats.

STEP 2
Under the ESA, the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service is authorized to identify and officially list organisms as either threatened or endangered. Once listed, these species receive strict legal protection, which includes bans on harming, harassing, or killing them, as well as restrictions on the sale, purchase, or transport of these organisms and any of their parts.

STEP 3
Beyond protection, the ESA also allows for the designation of critical habitats necessary for species recovery and enables the government to create recovery plans aimed at rebuilding healthy population levels. These tools make the ESA one of the most powerful conservation laws in the world.
