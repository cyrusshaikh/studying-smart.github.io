Chapter 16

Page 461: Exercise g

STEP 1
This question asks us to calculate two things:

• How much municipal solid waste (MSW) is left after composting and recycling major components
• How much money is saved on tipping fees as a result

STEP 2
We're told the following percentages of total MSW:

Food scraps: 12.5%
Paper and paperboard: 32.7%
Yard trimmings: 12.8%
Plastics: 12.1%
Glass: 5.3%

These materials together make up 75.4% of the waste stream.

Also provided:
Total MSW per year = 2.92 tons
Tipping fee = $60 per ton
Tipping cost without composting or recycling = $175/year

STEP 3
Now calculate the amount of waste diverted from landfilling:

2.92 tons × 0.754 = 2.20268 ≈ 2.2 tons
So, 2.2 tons of waste are removed from the landfill through composting and recycling.

STEP 4
Subtract this from the total to get the remaining waste sent to the landfill:

2.92 tons − 2.2 tons = 0.72 tons

STEP 5
Now calculate the updated tipping cost:

0.72 tons × $60 = $43.20

Then subtract to find the annual savings:
$175 − $43.20 = $131.80

Result:
Waste sent to landfill after composting/recycling = 0.72 tons
Tipping fee savings per year = $131.80
