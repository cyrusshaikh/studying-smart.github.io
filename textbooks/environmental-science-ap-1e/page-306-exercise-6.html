Chapter 11

Page 306: Exercise 6

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify which option does not represent a sustainable or traditional method of farming that supports long-term soil health and environmental balance.

STEP 2
Sustainable agriculture refers to farming practices that aim to produce food while preserving natural resources, especially soil, water, and biodiversity. These methods reduce the use of non-renewable inputs and help maintain soil fertility over time.

STEP 3
Several key practices that promote sustainable agriculture include:

Intercropping: Planting two or more complementary crops in the same field to enhance mutual growth. For example, legumes like peas can supply nitrogen to companion crops like corn.

Crop rotation: Alternating the types of crops grown in a particular field across different seasons to prevent nutrient depletion and improve soil structure.

Agroforestry: Integrating crops with trees and shrubs to reduce erosion, improve biodiversity, and create natural windbreaks.

Contour plowing: Used mainly on sloped land, this method involves plowing along natural elevation lines to reduce water runoff and minimize soil loss.

STEP 4
In contrast, nomadic herding involves the continuous movement of livestock in search of pasture. While it is an ancient practice, it is not aligned with sustainable farming goals. It often results in overgrazing, soil compaction, and increased risk of desertification, making it the least sustainable among the listed options.

Result: (a) — Nomadic herding is not a sustainable traditional farming technique.
