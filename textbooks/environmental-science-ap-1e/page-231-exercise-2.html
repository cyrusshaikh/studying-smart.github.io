Chapter 8

Page 231: Exercise 2

Step 1:
The interior of the Earth is made up of multiple distinct layers, each composed of materials with different chemical compositions and densities.

Step 2:
At the planet’s center lies the core, which is the deepest and densest part of Earth. It contains primarily iron and nickel, two of the heaviest elements found within the planet. The core has two parts: the outer core, which is liquid, and the inner core, which remains solid due to intense pressure.

Step 3:
Surrounding the core is the mantle, the thickest layer within Earth's structure. It contains dense rocks and molten material (magma), and lies between the core and the crust.

Step 4:
Beneath the lithosphere and within the upper mantle lies the asthenosphere. Although this layer is made of solid rock, the extreme heat and pressure allow the rocks to behave in a semi-liquid or plastic-like state, enabling slow movement over time.

Step 5:
Above the asthenosphere is the lithosphere, which includes the uppermost part of the mantle and the crust. This layer is rigid and brittle, and is broken into large segments called tectonic plates. Its chemical makeup is very different from that of the deeper layers.

Step 6:
Covering the surface is a thin layer known as soil, which supports all known life on the planet. This outermost layer contains essential nutrients and organic matter required for the development and survival of ecosystems.

Result: (c)
