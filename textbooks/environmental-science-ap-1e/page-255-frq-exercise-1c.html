Step 1

Scientists are concerned that these aquifers are being pumped in very large quantities (especially in recent decades). This depleted water is used for household drinks, for industry, agriculture, and others. The problem is that the exploitation of water is disproportionate to the speed of water renewal, ie. filling the tank. This filling is usually slow (it can take years, decades, or thousands of years for the tank to renew).

Step 2
2 of 3

Filling the tank also depends on its size. Consumption of water from closed aquifers is very high. Decreased water levels in these layers cause low water levels or complete drying of shallow water wells. Also, it may happen that for example, the water layer in Ogallala in the United States is facing over-exploitation.

Step 3
3 of 3

This will certainly drain the water to such an extent that if this level of emptying of the water layers in these plains continues, there will not be enough water during this century.
