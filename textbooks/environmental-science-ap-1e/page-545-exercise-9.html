Chapter 19

Page 545: Exercise 9

STEP 1
Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature caused primarily by the release of greenhouse gases through human activities such as fossil fuel combustion and deforestation.

STEP 2
One of the most apparent and measurable consequences of global warming is the accelerated melting of glaciers and polar ice caps, contributing significantly to rising sea levels.

STEP 3
Another major impact is the thawing of permafrost—ground that remains frozen for extended periods in polar regions. This thaw can release large amounts of greenhouse gases like methane, further intensifying warming.

STEP 4
Global warming also disrupts biological rhythms, altering flowering periods in plants and breeding seasons in animals. For instance, certain turtle species now lay eggs earlier in the year due to shifts in environmental cues.

STEP 5
While many of these effects have been observed and documented, one proposed consequence—the complete shutdown of thermohaline circulation in the oceans—has not yet occurred. This oceanic conveyor belt plays a vital role in regulating global climate but remains operational despite warming trends.

Result: (d)
