Exercise 2b

Step 1

Constructing dams requires a significant financial investment, especially when large volumes of water need to be stored. The greater the capacity of the reservoir, the larger and more structurally reinforced the dam must be—typically built from concrete, which adds to both material and labor costs.

Step 2

However, the cost of building a dam goes beyond just the economic aspect. There are major ecological and social consequences that often follow construction. These can include the forced displacement of communities, the submergence of fertile agricultural lands, disruption to wildlife habitats, and changes to the river’s natural flow.

Step 3

With over 845,000 dams built globally, the combined financial cost and environmental footprint is substantial. Many governments allocate extensive public funding for dam development, even though these projects often carry long-term trade-offs for ecosystems and nearby populations.
