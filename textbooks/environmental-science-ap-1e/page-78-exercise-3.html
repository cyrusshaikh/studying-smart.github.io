Chapter 3

Page 78: Exercise 3

STEP 1
To determine the total amount of water needed to irrigate a plantation of 900 mango trees, we must first calculate how much water is required per day for a single tree.

STEP 2
Watering needs vary depending on the season. During the hot, dry months, each tree requires 20 liters of water per day. Since the warm season typically lasts 6 months—or 180 days (6 × 30)—we’ll use this to calculate the total water required for one tree over that period.

STEP 3
Multiplying the daily water requirement by the number of days in the dry season:
20 L/day × 180 days = 3,600 L per tree

STEP 4
To find the total water needed for all 900 trees, we multiply the amount for one tree by the total number of trees:
3,600 L × 900 trees = 3,240,000 L

Result: (3,240,000 L)
