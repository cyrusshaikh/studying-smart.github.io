Chapter 11

Page 309: Exercise B

STEP 1
This question asks us to calculate the total ecological footprint for a person who consumes all of the listed food items over the course of one year.

STEP 2
To determine this, we simply add together the ecological footprints of each individual food item, as previously calculated:

Total Ecological Footprint = 0.6751 + 0.0048 + 0.098 + 0.1162 + 0.1665 + 0.0068 + 0.023 + 0.0183 + 0.052 + 0.035 = 1.1975 hectares/year
So, the total ecological footprint for a person consuming all the items is:

1.1975 hectares/person/year

RESULT
This value exceeds the global average ecological footprint, which is approximately 0.9 hectares/person/year, indicating a higher-than-average environmental impact.
