Exercise 2a

Step 1

Dams offer a wide range of benefits, particularly when it comes to flood control. By storing large volumes of water behind their walls, dams regulate river flow and reduce the risk of seasonal flooding downstream. This controlled release of water helps protect homes, infrastructure, farmland, and crops from damage caused by sudden surges.

Step 2

In addition to flood management, the water held in dams serves multiple practical purposes. The stored water’s potential energy is harnessed to generate electricity in hydroelectric plants. It is also distributed for public consumption and used in various industrial processes, making dams vital sources of both drinking water and industrial supply.

Step 3

Beyond their functional utility, dams also promote recreational and economic development. The artificial lakes they create often become sites for tourism and leisure activities such as boating, fishing, and swimming. Surrounding areas may develop new infrastructure and communities, and the formation of these lakes can support the emergence of entirely new aquatic ecosystems.
