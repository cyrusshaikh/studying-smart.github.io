Chapter 12

Page 340: Exercise 2e

Step 1
Because radioactive waste can potentially leak into the environment, it poses risks to ecosystems, wildlife, and human health. As a result, many nuclear-powered countries—including the United States—have struggled to establish a long-term, secure disposal site for this hazardous material.

Step 2
In the U.S., the Department of Energy began evaluating Yucca Mountain in Nevada as a potential permanent storage facility for high-level radioactive waste in 1978. By 2006, a comprehensive report affirmed the scientific credibility of the research supporting the site. However, the project was later halted due to changes in federal administration and policy direction, leaving the country without a finalized deep geological repository.
