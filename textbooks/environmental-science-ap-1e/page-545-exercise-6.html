Chapter 19

Page 545: Exercise 6

STEP 1
We’re asked to identify the accurate statement about global warming from the given choices.

STEP 2
Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth’s average temperature, primarily caused by human-induced emissions of greenhouse gases. These gases—like CO₂, CH₄, and N₂O—trap heat by absorbing infrared radiation emitted by Earth's surface, preventing it from escaping into space. This phenomenon intensifies the natural greenhouse effect and contributes to the warming of the planet.

STEP 3
Among the observable consequences of global warming are rising sea levels, increased frequency of extreme weather events, and accelerated melting of polar ice caps and glaciers.

STEP 4
However, it's also important to recognize that Earth has undergone many natural climate shifts in the past—including ice ages and warm interglacial periods—due to factors like changes in solar radiation, volcanic activity, and orbital variations. Acknowledging this context doesn’t contradict the fact that current warming trends are largely anthropogenic.

Result: (c)
