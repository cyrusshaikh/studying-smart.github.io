Chapter 17

Page 488: Exercise a

STEP 1
We’re asked to calculate how smoking affects the average lifespan of both men and women, based on the reduction in life expectancy provided for smokers.

STEP 2
According to the data:

The typical lifespan for men is 75.6 years, but smoking reduces it by 6.6 years.
75.6 − 6.6 = 69.0 years → Male smokers have a life expectancy of 69 years

For women, the average life expectancy is 80.8 years, and smoking shortens this by 3.9 years.
80.8 − 3.9 = 76.9 years → Female smokers have a life expectancy of 76.9 years

Result: Men who smoke: 69 years
Women who smoke: 76.9 years
