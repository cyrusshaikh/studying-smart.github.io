Chapter 8

Page 233: Exercise c

STEP 1
A major improvement to reduce environmental impact would be cutting back on how often we consume animal-based meals. For instance, if someone reduced their meat intake from seven meals a week to just three, it would help lower the overall demand for livestock. When this kind of dietary shift is adopted by many people, it reduces the number of animals being raised for food—which in turn decreases the amount of feed those animals require.

STEP 2
With fewer livestock to feed, the need to grow feed crops like corn, soybeans, and wheat also declines. This reduction would mean less use of synthetic fertilizers that are rich in minerals such as calcium, phosphorus, and magnesium—minerals that are mined from natural rock deposits. Cutting back on fertilizer use helps preserve these mineral resources and protects mountain ecosystems where these rocks are extracted, which benefits both plant and animal species living there.
