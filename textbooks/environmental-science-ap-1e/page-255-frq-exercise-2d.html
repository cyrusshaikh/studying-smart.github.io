Exercise 2d

Step 1

Before moving forward with a dam project, decision-makers must carefully weigh both the potential benefits and the environmental and financial costs. This evaluation process involves analyzing a range of variables—such as construction expenses, expected utility, and possible environmental degradation. When feasible, negative impacts like ecosystem disruption, relocation, or pollution are quantified in monetary terms to enable a direct cost-benefit comparison.

Step 2

A major component of this analysis is conducting an environmental impact assessment, which examines how the dam might affect local ecosystems, biodiversity, and water quality. If the projected benefits—such as improved water supply, energy generation, or flood control—outweigh the long-term ecological and economic drawbacks, the construction of the dam can be considered justified. In such cases, public interest and utility often take precedence.
