Chapter 6

Page 176: Exercise 2a

STEP 1
Western lowland gorillas display characteristics typical of a K-selected species. These animals live long lives—up to around 40 years—and reproduce slowly. Males reach sexual maturity around age 15, and females around age 8. Females typically reproduce only once every four years, and offspring take a long time to mature, meaning population turnover is very slow.

STEP 2
Because of this slow reproductive cycle and delayed maturity, gorillas experience very gradual population growth. Even though they may have access to ample natural resources like food, habitat, and shelter, their populations still increase at a slow rate. They remain near the carrying capacity of their environment and are among the slowest-growing populations in the mammal world.
