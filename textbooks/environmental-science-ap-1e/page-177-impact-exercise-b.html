Chapter 6

Page 177: Exercise B

STEP 1
Analysis of the environmental index graph, derived from tabular data, shows that the tropical regions experienced the most significant decline in biodiversity. From 1970 to 2005, the biodiversity index in these areas steadily decreased, with a small drop of 1% between 2000 and 2005—falling from 0.55 to 0.54.

STEP 2
When comparing temperate and tropical zones, it becomes clear that tropical ecosystems underwent a much steeper reduction in species diversity. Although biodiversity levels in both regions were similar around 1985 (with indices near 0.91 for tropical and 0.90 for temperate areas), a sharp contrast developed in the years that followed.

STEP 3
Between 1985 and 2005, the biodiversity index for tropical areas continued to decline sharply, reaching 0.54 by 2005. In contrast, the temperate zone saw a slight increase, followed by a modest decline, stabilizing around 1.04. This indicates that while biodiversity fluctuated slightly in temperate regions, it consistently declined in the tropics throughout the observed period.
