Chapter 5

Page 143: Measuring Your Impact Exercise E

STEP 1
The data indicate that current investment in biodiversity conservation is quite limited. However, if lawns were maintained on only 90% of their total area, significant annual savings could be achieved—not just in financial costs but also in energy consumption, such as gasoline, which could then be redirected to other uses.

STEP 2
Reducing lawn maintenance also means using fewer agrochemicals like herbicides, which are known to reduce plant biodiversity and contribute to soil pollution. Less reliance on these chemicals brings dual benefits: it cuts costs and helps protect the environment from chemical contamination.

STEP 3
This decrease in agrochemical and fuel use results in lower pollution overall—both from the production processes of these substances and their application, which affects air quality (through fuel combustion) and soil health. Moreover, enhancing biodiversity on even 10% of grassland areas would encourage a variety of animal species, such as insects and small mammals, creating new habitats within a richer ecosystem.
