Page 568: Exercise 5

STEP 1
In environmental and ecological economics, valuation refers to the process of assigning a monetary value to non-market resources and ecological services—such as biodiversity, scenic beauty, and natural ecosystems—that typically don't have a direct price.

STEP 2
To evaluate the worth of a protected natural area, economists have developed a few approaches:

One approach estimates value based on direct economic activity, such as the revenue generated from entrance fees or tourism at the site.

Another approach involves contingent valuation, which uses surveys to ask individuals how much they would be willing to pay simply to know that a species or ecosystem exists—regardless of whether they will personally experience it.

STEP 3
From this perspective, income earned by increasing labor to boost production does not qualify as a valid measure of environmental valuation.

Result: (d)
