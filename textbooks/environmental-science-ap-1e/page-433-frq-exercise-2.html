Chapter 15

Page 433: Exercise 2

STEP 1
a) A 2003 editorial in Medical News Today discussed the link between increased use of air conditioning systems in office environments and the development of Sick Building Syndrome (SBS). This condition arises when microorganisms accumulate within HVAC systems, particularly in the ventilation ducts, and trigger symptoms such as eye and throat irritation, dizziness, respiratory issues, and other health complaints among occupants.

STEP 2
One method that has proven effective in reducing these symptoms is Ultraviolet Germicidal Irradiation (UVGI). By targeting and eliminating bacteria, molds, and other microbes in ventilation systems, UVGI use led to significant health improvements:

A 20% overall decrease in reported symptoms
A 40% drop in respiratory-related complaints
A 30% reduction in reports of nasal congestion

STEP 3
b) Another contributor to SBS is the presence of chemical pollutants, particularly volatile organic compounds (VOCs). These substances often come from building materials, furniture, and cleaning products. However, it's important to note that UVGI systems do not address VOCs, as they are designed to kill microorganisms—not remove gases or chemical vapors.

STEP 4
In cases where the sources of indoor air contamination are clearly identified and manageable, the most effective solution is often removing or modifying the source itself—this includes switching materials, improving ventilation, or changing cleaning practices.

STEP 5
c) UVGI technology may also be practical in developing countries, thanks to its relatively low installation costs. By reducing the spread of illness and improving indoor air quality, it can also help organizations save money by lowering healthcare costs and reducing employee sick days.
