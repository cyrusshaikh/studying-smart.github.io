Chapter 9

Page 255: Exercise C

STEP 1
Let’s first calculate the annual water use for a standard modern toilet that uses 6 liters per flush, assuming 4 flushes per day:

6 L × 4 flushes/day × 365 days = 8,760 liters per year

STEP 2
Now consider upgrading the same toilet to a dual-flush system:

1 full flush per day at 6 L
3 light flushes at 3 L each

Daily water use:
(1 × 6 L) + (3 × 3 L) = 15 L/day
Annual water use:
15 L/day × 365 days = 5,475 liters per year

STEP 3
Subtracting the upgraded system’s usage from the regular system:

8,760 L − 5,475 L = 3,285 liters saved per year per person by switching to a dual-flush toilet.
