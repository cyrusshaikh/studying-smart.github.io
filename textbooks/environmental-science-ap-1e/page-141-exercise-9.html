Chapter 5

Page 141: Exercise 9

STEP 1
Species in any ecosystem can tolerate certain abiotic factors such as temperature, salinity, pH, and humidity, but only within specific limits. When these conditions remain within tolerable ranges, species can adapt, survive, and thrive in their habitats.

STEP 2
However, if these abiotic factors exceed those limits—such as extreme heat or high salinity—they can have harmful effects on species. This may cause population declines, reproductive failure, or even extinction if individuals cannot cope with the changes.

STEP 3
In this way, abiotic factors like sunlight, temperature, humidity, precipitation, soil, and water play a crucial role in determining which species live where and influence whether they can survive when environmental conditions change.
