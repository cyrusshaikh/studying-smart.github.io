Chapter 16

Page 459: Exercise 2

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify which listed option is not a beneficial or intended result of incinerating municipal solid waste (MSW).

STEP 2
Municipal solid waste refers to everyday trash generated by households—such as food scraps, paper, packaging, and furniture—that is collected and processed by local waste management services.

STEP 3
One common method of handling MSW is incineration, which involves burning the waste at high temperatures to significantly reduce its mass and volume. Because household waste typically contains carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen, it burns easily under controlled conditions.

STEP 4
When done properly, incineration not only reduces the physical amount of waste—thereby extending the lifespan of landfills—but it can also be used to generate heat, energy, and electricity, depending on the composition of the material burned.

STEP 5
However, a known drawback of incineration is that it can increase air pollution due to the release of harmful gases and particulate matter. Since this is not a desired effect, the correct answer is option (d).

Result: (d)
