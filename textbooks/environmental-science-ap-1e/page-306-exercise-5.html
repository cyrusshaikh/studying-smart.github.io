STEP 1
This question asks you to identify how the Green Revolution contributed to increased global food production.

STEP 2
The Green Revolution marked a major turning point in agriculture by introducing modern tools, technologies, and scientific methods that dramatically boosted crop yields and farming efficiency.

STEP 3
Several major changes during this era led to higher productivity:

Mechanization: Farmers began using machines instead of manual labor, which allowed for faster and more efficient planting, harvesting, and processing of crops.

Advanced irrigation techniques: Improvements in water management systems ensured that crops received sufficient water, even in areas prone to drought or irregular rainfall.

Chemical fertilizers: The widespread use of synthetic fertilizers provided crops with essential nutrients, significantly increasing their growth and output.

STEP 4
Monocropping became common, allowing farmers to focus on one high-yield crop over large areas, which simplified management and maximized profits.

Improved crop varieties: Scientists developed high-yield, disease-resistant seeds that required fewer inputs and could thrive under various conditions.
