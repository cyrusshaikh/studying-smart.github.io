Chapter 13

Page 375: Exercise 1d

Step 1
Two economic benefits of generating electricity through hydroelectric power are:

  • Once operational, hydroelectric plants offer lower electricity costs to consumers compared to power stations fueled by natural gas or nuclear energy.
  • These plants require very little fossil fuel during ongoing operation, helping reduce long-term fuel expenditures.

Step 2
Two environmental disadvantages of hydroelectric power generation are:

  • Building a hydroelectric facility typically involves damming a naturally flowing river, which can lead to the flooding of extensive lowland areas or landscapes of ecological, aesthetic, or cultural value.
  • Dams disrupt the natural flow patterns of rivers, which can significantly alter downstream ecosystems—impacting aquatic habitats, altering flood cycles, and affecting both wildlife and local communities.
