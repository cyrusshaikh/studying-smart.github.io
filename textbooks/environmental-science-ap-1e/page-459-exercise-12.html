Chapter 16

Page 459: Exercise 12

STEP 1
This question asks us to determine which gas is produced in landfills during the anaerobic breakdown of organic waste.

STEP 2
When food scraps, paper, and other organic materials begin to break down in landfills, they are first exposed to oxygen. In this early aerobic stage, bacteria consume the waste and generate small amounts of gases. Once the oxygen is depleted, the process shifts to anaerobic decomposition—a stage where oxygen is no longer present.

STEP 3
During this oxygen-free breakdown, methane (CH₄) becomes a major byproduct, along with some carbon dioxide. Methane is a potent greenhouse gas and is one of the most significant environmental concerns related to landfill emissions.

Result: (c)
