Page 82: Exercise 5

STEP 1
The primary source of energy in an ecosystem is solar radiation. Producers—organisms at the first trophic level—convert this solar energy into chemical energy stored in organic molecules. That energy is then passed along the food chain, from producers to consumers and eventually to decomposers.

STEP 2
Producers capture inorganic elements from the non-living environment, such as carbon dioxide and water, and use sunlight to carry out photosynthesis. This process allows them to form organic compounds, which serve as food and energy sources for other organisms in the ecosystem.

STEP 3
Primary consumers, also known as herbivores, eat the producers and absorb the energy stored in their organic compounds. This energy is then passed along to higher-level consumers (like carnivores) through feeding relationships, continuing up the food chain until it reaches the decomposers.

STEP 4
Decomposers break down the remains of dead organisms, releasing inorganic materials back into the soil. Producers then reuse these nutrients during photosynthesis, completing the cycle of energy and matter flow in the ecosystem.
