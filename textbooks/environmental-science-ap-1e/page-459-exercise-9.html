Chapter 16

Page 459: Exercise 9

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify which category of items makes up the largest portion by weight of municipal solid waste (MSW) in the United States, based on 2008 data.

STEP 2
MSW consists of everyday discarded materials—like food waste, paper, packaging, and broken goods—collected by local governments. A portion of this waste is recycled, but the rest is disposed of in landfills or incinerators.

STEP 3
Looking at Figure 16.5(b), the largest share of MSW by weight comes from containers and packaging materials. These items often include large quantities of paper products, which make up a significant part of the waste stream—especially when not recycled.

STEP 4
In comparison, e-waste (like electronics and media devices) accounts for only 2%, while durable goods (such as tires and appliances) contribute around 18%. Containers and packaging, however, are responsible for about 31% of total MSW by weight.

STEP 5
Therefore, the correct answer is option (a) — packaging contributes the most to the overall mass of municipal solid waste.

Result: (a)
