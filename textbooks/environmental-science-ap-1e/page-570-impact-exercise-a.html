Chapter 20

Page 570: Exercise a

STEP 1
To determine how the ecological footprint and GDP per capita changed between 1970 and 2000, we use the formula:
Percent Change = ((New Value − Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. We’ll apply this formula for both the ecological footprint (measured in hectares per person) and GDP per capita (in dollars) for each country.

STEP 2 – China
Ecological footprint: ((1.5 − 0.7) / 0.7) × 100 = 114.29%
GDP per capita: ((1200 − 100) / 100) × 100 = 1100%

STEP 3 – India
Ecological footprint: ((0.7 − 0.7) / 0.7) × 100 = 0%
GDP per capita: ((480 − 220) / 220) × 100 ≈ 118.18%

STEP 4 – Japan
Ecological footprint: ((4.3 − 2.5) / 2.5) × 100 = 72%
GDP per capita: ((37000 − 15000) / 15000) × 100 ≈ 146.67%

STEP 5 – United States
Ecological footprint: ((9.3 − 6.1) / 6.1) × 100 ≈ 52.46%
GDP per capita: ((34000 − 18000) / 18000) × 100 ≈ 88.89%

Result:
