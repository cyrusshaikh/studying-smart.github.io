Chapter 15

Page 434: Exercise a

STEP 1
This question asks us to calculate how much mercury is released per year from burning 1.5 million metric tons (MMT) of coal, using three different types of coal with varying mercury concentrations.

STEP 2
We are provided the following data:

Mercury concentration in average U.S. coal: 0.1 μg/g
Mercury concentration in high-mercury coal: 0.5 μg/g
Mercury concentration in low-mercury coal: 0.04 μg/g

Annual coal usage: 1.5 million metric tons (MMT)
1 metric ton = 1,000,000 grams
So, 1.5 MMT = 1.5 × 10¹² grams

STEP 3
Typical U.S. Coal:
Mercury concentration = 0.1 μg per gram = 0.1 × 10⁻⁶ g/g
Total mercury released = (0.1 × 10⁻⁶ g/g) × (1.5 × 10¹² g coal) = 1.5 × 10⁵ grams of mercury
Convert to tons: 1.5 × 10⁵ g = 0.15 tons
So, burning 1.5 MMT of average coal emits 0.15 tons of mercury per year.

STEP 4
High-Mercury Coal:
Mercury concentration = 0.5 μg/g = 0.5 × 10⁻⁶ g/g
Total mercury = (0.5 × 10⁻⁶) × (1.5 × 10¹²) = 7.5 × 10⁵ grams
Convert to tons: 7.5 × 10⁵ g = 0.75 tons
Burning 1.5 MMT of high-mercury coal emits 0.75 tons of mercury annually.

STEP 5
Low-Mercury Coal:
Mercury concentration = 0.04 μg/g = 0.04 × 10⁻⁶ g/g
Total mercury = (0.04 × 10⁻⁶) × (1.5 × 10¹²) = 6.0 × 10⁴ grams
Convert to tons: 6.0 × 10⁴ g = 0.06 tons
So, 1.5 MMT of low-mercury coal would emit 0.06 tons of mercury per year.

STEP 6
Final Table:

Coal Type – Mercury Concentration (μg/g) – Amount Used (MMT) – Mercury Emitted (tons/year)
U.S. Average – 0.1 – 1.5 – 0.15
High-Mercury – 0.5 – 1.5 – 0.75
Low-Mercury – 0.04 – 1.5 – 0.06

Result: (a)
