Chapter 3

Page 84: Free-Response – Exercise 2b

STEP 1
In contrast, Mr. Jared takes a more holistic and environmentally responsible perspective. He considers the broader, long-term consequences that could result from unregulated deforestation, particularly when trees are removed without consideration for location, quantity, or ecological impact.

STEP 2
One of his primary concerns is the potential for groundwater contamination. The area’s wells, which serve nearby landowners, rely on surrounding forests to maintain water quality. Trees act as natural filters, helping to purify rainwater as it infiltrates the soil and enters underground aquifers. Without this natural filtration system, the risk of water pollution increases significantly.

STEP 3
Deforestation would also threaten local biodiversity, displacing both plant life and wildlife that depend on the forest for shelter and survival. The removal of habitat would destabilize the region’s ecological balance, disrupting essential processes within the ecosystem. Because this forest is part of a much larger ecological network, any major disturbance—such as habitat destruction or increased runoff—could lead to further environmental consequences, including soil erosion, degraded water systems, and reduced biodiversity.
