Page 23 – Exercise 8

Free-Response Question

Step 1

While humans have successfully helped bring back certain endangered species—such as the American bison, bald eagle, peregrine falcon, and American alligator—many other species, like the snow leopard, continue to face serious population decline and remain at risk of extinction, requiring protective intervention.

Step 2

The leading causes behind the decline and extinction of these animals include habitat destruction, overhunting, resource exploitation, and the impacts of climate change. Many species have disappeared because they were unable to adapt to the rapidly changing environmental conditions.

Step 3

Additionally, many animals live in regions with rich, fertile land. Unfortunately, humans often clear these areas for agriculture or development, destroying natural habitats in the process. As a result, countless species lose their homes and struggle to survive. Scientific studies estimate that around 10,000 species go extinct each year.
