Chapter 12

Page 339: Exercise 2

STEP 1
This question asks us to identify why energy conversion during electricity generation from fuels is inefficient.

STEP 2
The primary reason lies in the Second Law of Thermodynamics, which explains that in any energy conversion process, some energy is inevitably lost—usually as heat. This law states that entropy (disorder) increases in any natural process, and energy tends to move from areas of high temperature to low temperature, making complete conversion into usable work impossible.

STEP 3
While the Hubbert curve relates to oil production trends—specifically predicting peak oil output—it doesn't explain conversion inefficiency. It shows two projected limits (upper and lower) for oil reserves over time but is unrelated to thermodynamics.

STEP 4
The First Law of Thermodynamics, also known as the Law of Energy Conservation, states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another. However, it doesn't account for the inefficiency or loss during the conversion process.

STEP 5
The Law of Limiting Factors refers to biological and chemical reactions, where the rate of a process is limited by the scarcest resource. It is unrelated to energy conversion efficiency in power generation.

Result: ✅ Correct Answer: (d) — The Second Law of Thermodynamics explains the low efficiency in energy conversion from fuels to electricity.
