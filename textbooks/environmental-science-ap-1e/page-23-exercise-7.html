Page 23 – Exercise 7

Free-Response Question

Step 1

To begin, we examine the graph and identify the amount of grain (in kilograms) produced per person for each year listed. We’ll then look at how these values change every ten years from 1950 to 2000—creating five separate time intervals.

Step 2

Next, we calculate the percentage increase or decrease in grain production per person for each of those five decades. This helps us understand the rate of change during each period.

Step 3

Once we’ve calculated all five percentage changes, we’ll determine the average percent increase in grain production per person over the full 50-year span.

Step 4

To do this, we’ll find the arithmetic mean of the five percentages. The original values from the graph are as follows:

  • 1950: 250 kg
  • 1960: 260 kg
  • 1970: 280 kg
  • 1980: 325 kg
  • 1990: 305 kg
  • 2000: 310 kg

Step 5

Calculating the change in kilograms:

  • 1950–1960: +10 kg
  • 1960–1970: +20 kg
  • 1970–1980: +45 kg
  • 1980–1990: −20 kg
  • 1990–2000: +5 kg

Step 6

Adding these values together:
10 + 20 + 45 − 20 + 5 = 60 kg total increase over the 50-year period.

Step 7

Now we compute the percent change for each decade using the standard percentage formula.

Step 8

1950–1960:
(10 ÷ 250) × 100 = 4%

Step 9

1960–1970:
(20 ÷ 260) × 100 = 7.69%

Step 10

1970–1980:
(45 ÷ 280) × 100 = 16.07%

Step 11

1980–1990:
(−20 ÷ 305) × 100 = −6.55%

Step 12

1990–2000:
(5 ÷ 305) × 100 = 1.63%

Step 13

Now we sum up the percentage changes:
4 + 7.69 + 16.07 − 6.55 + 1.63 = 22.84%

This indicates that over the 50-year span from 1950 to 2000, the total percentage increase in grain production per person was approximately 22.8%.

Result
