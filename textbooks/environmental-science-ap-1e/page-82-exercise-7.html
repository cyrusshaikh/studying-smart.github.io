Chapter 3

Page 82: Exercise 7

STEP 1
In the African Serengeti, the base of the food chain—the first trophic level—is made up of producers such as grasses and shrubs. Above them, at the second trophic level, are primary consumers like zebras, gazelles, wildebeest, goats, and bulls that feed directly on the vegetation. At the third trophic level are the secondary consumers, including predators like lions, tigers, and cheetahs, which feed on the herbivores and gain energy originally captured by the producers.

STEP 2
For instance, in the Serengeti, abundant grasses and shrubs are consumed by grazing animals like zebras and gazelles. These herbivores, in turn, are preyed upon by carnivores such as lions and cheetahs.

STEP 3
Plant life in the Serengeti is far more plentiful than the animals that feed on it. Additionally, there are more herbivores (primary consumers) than there are predators (secondary consumers), forming a typical pyramid structure.

STEP 4
This means the ecosystem contains the largest number of producers, a smaller population of herbivores, and the fewest number of carnivores at the top of the food chain.
