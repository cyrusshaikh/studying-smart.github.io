Question:

State two possible sources of error in the process of making the solar cell.

Answer:

  1. Uneven TiO₂ Layer: If the titanium dioxide coating on the glass slide is applied unevenly or too thickly, it can reduce the surface area available for dye absorption and hinder electron movement, lowering the efficiency of the solar cell.
  2. Incomplete Dye Absorption: If the TiO₂ layer is not given enough time to fully absorb the dye, fewer dye molecules will be available to capture light energy, which directly reduces the cell’s ability to generate electricity.
  3. Poor Sealing of the Cell: If the two glass slides are not sealed tightly, the electrolyte solution can leak or evaporate. This disrupts the internal chemical cycle, leading to lower current or complete failure of the cell.
  4. Contamination on Glass Slides: Any dirt, oil, or residue on the glass slides—especially on the conductive side—can interfere with electron flow or block light from reaching the dye, both of which can reduce the performance of the solar cell.
