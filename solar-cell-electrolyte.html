Electrolyte Role

Question: What was the purpose of adding the electrolyte solution to the cell, and what would happen if you did not have enough of the electrolyte? Please explain.
The electrolyte plays a crucial role in keeping the solar cell functioning properly. After the dye absorbs sunlight and transfers an electron to the titanium dioxide (TiO₂) layer, the dye becomes positively charged. To keep the process going, the electrolyte provides an electron to the dye, allowing it to return to its original state so it can absorb more sunlight and repeat the cycle. Without enough electrolyte, this regeneration process would slow down or stop completely. As a result, fewer electrons would be transferred, and the current produced by the solar cell would decrease significantly.
